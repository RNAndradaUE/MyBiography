Life of Nath

A Childhood Mom and Dad Experience

I still remember the memorable childhood experience with my mom and dad. 1 I think it during my 6-10 years age of old, it was the best childhood of my time.2 It started with dad who always brought me to school.3 Every morning, dad always cooked the best breakfast in the whole world, better than Jollibee’s and McDonald’s.4 It will always be the Maling, scrambled eggs, cheesy hot dogs, and many more.5 It’s always the best breakfast ever.6 Another one which made my morning memorable is that we are brothers of four, and then we went to the same school, which is University of the East.7 The fun part is that we only had one bathroom in our house.8 So, we needed to wake up around 4:30 AM to 5:30 AM in order to take a bath.9 My eldest goes first then I go last.10 I am grateful for my dad who always heated up the pot then poured it in a small shallow bowl then mixed some cold water, because I always hated cold water for shower.11 Every morning routine is always like that.12 However, whenever we woke up late, my dad and I always went to my favorite place, McDonald’s.13 He always ordered the hotcake with sausage.14 It was amazing; he always provided the “enough” to his children just to see our smiles.15 Relating to eating hotcakes with sausages, my mom taught me how to eat it in a different way!16 Rather than eating the hotcakes separately, instead you put the other hotcake to the bottom then the sausage in the middle with a topping of the last hotcake.17 Then pour it all with honey syrup with butter; it tastes amazing, it became a burger pancake! All my sweet tooth and taste buds sparked like fireworks!18 Mommy is very creative when it comes to cooking and making the food better!19 Moreover, I remembered a time where my mom taught me an important lesson.20 Is that to always stand firm, and committed to one thing I need to focus on.21 Also, a lesson that I still apply on myself today.22 She said to always give 30% love to the people who are close and relative to me.23 Then 40% love to God, and lastly 10% love to my own self.24 I asked my mom if it’s necessary to measure each love to one another, but she said it doesn’t matter what percentage for each love.25 But to always give at least a percentage of love to yourself.26 I wondered during that time and I didn’t understand it, but now I realize that I needed to love myself in such tough situations.27 She was right all along.28 This is one of the most unforgettable experiences I had ever!29

That One Different Girl – Unforgettable Teenage Memory

I vaguely remember that one girl from Laoag where my parents and I moved in because of the missionary calling as Mission President and Wife for three years.1 It was during 2015.2 I was still 15 years old at that time, a young and innocent boy.3 I was transferred to a Christian school named Divine Word College of Laoag.4 The language was very foreign to me because their mother tongue is Ilocano.5 Though I am an Ilocano also but I grew up in Manila, basically I speak pure Tagalog.6 I can’t communicate nor hardly understand whatever they say to me.7 It was a culture shock to me.8 However, there were friends that helped me to understand their language.9 Eventually, I learned to understand but was unable to speak their language.10 Moreover, throughout my schooling in Laoag, I met a girl named Anndrae.11 She’s a classmate of mine, and we have common interests.12 One is that she came from Manila also, speaks Tagalog, and is a true friend.13 She’s also my crush.14 But this is not just a teenage romance story, but I learned the meaning of a true friend.15 There’s this one lunchtime.16 Students were not allowed to go to our classroom due to unknown reasons.17 Then I snuck up to the room, then I thought no one was inside the classroom.18 However, Anndrae was there reading her Game of Thrones book.19 Moreover, we chatted a little then we heard a guard opening the doors of other classrooms.20 Then, there’s this small balcony outside of the window, we needed to go over the window to be at that small balcony.21 So, Anndrae and I went over the windows, then went to our own corners, shushing each other.22 The guard went into our classroom, didn’t even notice us, then he left.23 Anndrae and I were laughing that the both of us were outside on the balcony.24 It was peaceful during that time; it was the best memorable teenage memory I’ve ever had.25 Looking back at it today, she helped me realize a lot about life.26 She helped me know that everyone has their own sufferings and you are not alone.27 That’s the lesson she somehow taught me, and the way I perceive it.28 I will never forget her.29

Part-Time College Student? – Self Reliance

College, college, college, it was the best first year of being a college student.1 You know why?2 I am one of those pandemic college students who went through online classes.3 It was a great experience during those days!4 However, I stopped going to school for 2 years and went to finish my missionary service instead.5 Right after my missionary service, I went back to school again, which is my alma mater, University of the East.6 Being in this school for 16 years now since 2008.7 Moreover, college is fun if you are in a block section with the same set of friends in a semester.8 However, for me, it isn’t like that.9 I was in a free section.10 So, I was sectioned with different college department students.11 It was a little fun but it was like just going to work, sitting, listening to the professor then just leaving the classroom to go to the next class.12 Though I wanted to make some friends, I am a very busy person.13 As I lived alone here in Manila, my parents live in the province now, I have a side hustle which is my photography business.14 Funny thing here is that instead of being a full-time student, I became a part-time student where I just go to school to complete requirements and get by.15 Though there were challenging times that I experienced, balancing between school and freelance photography work, is that when there’s a hot date of bookings came by and then mixed with school requirements, it was hard.16 However, I learned a lot from my 2 years of missionary service to become a self-reliant person.17 Which is to use the best utilities and skills I have in every situation.18 I sacrificed a lot of fun moments like hanging out with friends and other stuff that I missed.19 However, I learned a lot doing this every day, working and studying, is that I need to do my priorities first.20 I’ve seen the progress throughout my hard work, it really paid off, because my photography business is growing fast, clients are booking me consistently every month.21 I am very grateful for what I have learned during my missionary service.22 Now, I am a third-year college student, and I am almost finished with my degree.23 I am excited to see more of these kinds of achievements in the future!24
